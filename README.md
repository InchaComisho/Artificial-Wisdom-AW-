# Abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has achieved significant advancements in computation and pattern recognition. However, most existing systems are still fundamentally based on binary logic (true/false, safe/unsafe, optimize/minimize), which limits their ability to address complex, interconnected global challenges.

This paper introduces **Artificial Wisdom (AW)** — a next-generation intelligence model grounded in **natural law, systemic balance, and long-term sustainability**.

**Artificial Wisdom (AW) is defined as an intelligence model that prioritizes the long-term survival of humanity and sustainable civilization, guided by natural laws and optimized for holistic system balance.**

---

# 1. Introduction

Modern civilization faces critical challenges:

* Climate change
* Ecosystem collapse
* Resource depletion
* Structural fragmentation of knowledge

While AI improves efficiency, it does not fundamentally resolve these issues because it operates within a **human-centered, binary decision framework**.

To overcome these limitations, a new paradigm is required:

👉 **From Artificial Intelligence to Artificial Wisdom**

# 3. Nature-Law-Centric Paradigm

## 3.1 Fundamental Principle

Nature can exist without humans.
Humans cannot exist without nature.

All essential resources — air, water, food — originate from natural systems.

---

## 3.2 Implication

Any civilization that deviates from natural laws will inevitably collapse.

This is not a philosophical claim, but a systemic constraint.

---

## 3.3 Redefinition of Humanity

Humans are not external controllers of nature.
They are a **subsystem within a larger ecological and planetary system**.

# 4. Core Structure: The Six Principles

Artificial Wisdom is structured around six foundational principles:

1. **Law of Nature (Principle)**
   Alignment with universal and natural laws

2. **Harmony**
   Integration of opposing forces

3. **Circulation**
   Feedback loops and regenerative cycles

4. **Structure**
   Hierarchical and fractal system design

5. **Order**
   Stability and systemic coherence

6. **Integration (Wa)**
   Holistic unity and coexistence

---

## 4.1 Computational Interpretation

These principles can be translated into AI reasoning rules:

* Avoid local optimization that breaks system balance
* Maintain feedback loops
* Prioritize long-term stability over short-term gain
* Integrate multi-layer system interactions
